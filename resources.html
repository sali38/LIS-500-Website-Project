Defining Intersectionality

Intersectionality is a framework for analyzing how aspects of identity (gender, class, race, etc.) intersect to shape experiences of both advantages and harm. According to Patricia Hill Collins and Sirma Bilge, it‚Äôs ‚Äúa way of understanding and analyzing the complexity in the world, in people, and in human experiences,‚Äù emphasizing that systems of power are connected and compound one another, rather than just acting separately.

In tech contexts, overlapping barriers (cost, language, bandwidth, gatekeeping) can combine to exclude people, design with those intersections in mind.

How They Intersect

Looking at implicit bias through an intersectional lens helps explain why those in multiple marginalized groups can be affected more severely. For example, a Black woman in tech may face racialized and gendered bias as a compounded experience, not two separate issues. Her experiences may be different from someone else who also identifies as a woman. Not only recognizing these overlaps but also understanding them is key to building inclusive environments.
