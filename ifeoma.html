Ifeoma Ozoma is a policy expert, equity advocate, and one of the most recognized whistleblowers in Silicon Valley. She has worked at major companies including Google, Facebook, and Pinterest, where she publicly spoke out against racism, gender discrimination, and pay inequity.

In 2020, Ozoma helped expose discriminatory practices at Pinterest ‚Äî an act that brought national attention to inequities in tech workplaces. Beyond her personal experience, she co-authored the landmark Silenced No More Act (2021) in California, preventing companies from using NDAs to silence workers who report harassment or discrimination.

Ozoma later launched the Tech Worker Handbook , a free resource offering guidance for workers navigating contracts, whistleblowing, legal retaliation, and media interactions. It has since become a widely used toolkit for tech employees seeking transparency and safety.

Her story exemplifies intersectionality. As a Black woman in tech, Ozoma faced overlapping systems of bias ‚Äî showing how inequity is not a single-axis issue. Her activism challenges tech culture to address power structures, not just diversity statistics.

Today, Ozoma runs the consulting firm Earthseed, advising organizations on equity, worker rights, and accountability in digital environments. Her work continues to shape policies that protect employees and challenge corporate power.
